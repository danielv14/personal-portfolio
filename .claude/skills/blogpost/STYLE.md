# Style guide

This is the voice calibration for blog posts. It exists because AI-drafted posts
tend to converge on a polished, reflective essay voice — and after enough posts,
"read the recent posts for tone" just feeds that voice back into itself. Calibrate
against these rules and the reference posts below, not against whatever the last
ten posts happen to sound like.

## The voice

Personal, practical, a bit playful. The older posts on this blog had jokes,
parentheses, self-deprecation and casual asides ("General Zod has entered the
game", "Skämt och sido", "automagiskt", "massa fulkod med andra ord"). That
looseness is a feature, not a flaw to polish away. A post should sound like a
developer telling a colleague about something over coffee — not like an essay.

## Hard rules for drafts

These are the AI tells that crept into posts written between late 2025 and mid
2026. Check every draft against them:

1. **Max one "Inte X, utan Y" construction per post.** ("Inte som ett experiment,
   utan som...", "Inte skisser, utan tre kompletta migreringar"). It's effective
   once. It was appearing in every other post.
2. **No formulaic dramatic one-liners as turning points.** "Så jag testade en
   grej." / "Den här gången gjorde jag något annat." / "Nu skriver jag inte alls."
   One short punchy paragraph is fine if the moment earns it — but it had become
   the default transition in every post.
3. **Don't end on an aphoristic zoom-out by default.** "Det ersätter inte omdöme,
   men det ger omdömet bättre underlag." Nearly every post was ending with a tidy
   quotable reflection. It's okay for a post to just... end. Casually. With a
   loose thread, a list, or a "vi får se".
4. **Vary sentence rhythm.** Long rambling sentence, then a short one, a
   parenthetical aside (like this), a question. Uniform medium-length sentences
   read as generated.
5. **Short posts (under ~600 words) rarely need subheadings.** A two-sentence
   section under an H2 is a sign the structure is wearing the content. Let prose
   carry short posts.
6. **Prefer one concrete artifact when the post is about something built.** A code
   block, a config snippet, an actual prompt, a command. The older posts were
   tutorials with real code; don't let every post become a code-free reflection.
7. **Preamble must add something the summary doesn't already say.** If it
   restates the summary in different words, drop it.
8. **Keep the humor in.** If a joke or a self-deprecating aside comes up in
   conversation, it probably belongs in the post.
